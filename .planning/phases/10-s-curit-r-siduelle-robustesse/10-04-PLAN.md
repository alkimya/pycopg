<objective>
Close the residual of B2 (SEC-02, decision D-02): SEC-02's exception-masking is already satisfied (cursor() handles transaction status; session() already re-raises without suppressing and resets `_session_conn`). The REMAINING defect is a connection LEAK: in `session()` (`database.py` ~380-392) `self._session_conn.commit()` and `self._session_conn.close()` live in the SAME `try`, so if `commit()` raises, `close()` is skipped and the connection leaks. The async pendant (`async_database.py` ~215-225) has the identical shape. Restructure the `finally` (sync AND async) so `close()` ALWAYS runs even when `commit()` raises, the commit exception still propagates, and `_session_conn` is reset to None. Add a dedicated red->green regression test.

Purpose: Prevent connection-pool/handle exhaustion when a session commit fails.
Output: Fixed `pycopg/database.py` + `pycopg/async_database.py`, `tests/test_session_leak.py`.
</objective>

Target behavior: in the outer `finally`, FIRST attempt `if not autocommit: self._session_conn.commit()` inside its own `try`, and in a `finally` paired with that commit attempt, call `self._session_conn.close()` — so close() runs whether or not commit() raised. Do NOT change the autocommit short-circuit semantics (when `autocommit=True`, skip commit but STILL close and STILL reset `_session_conn`).

Apply the identical restructure to `AsyncDatabase.session()` (async_database.py ~215-225) using `await self._session_conn.commit()` / `await self._session_conn.close()`. The async side is the mirror of the sync side; keep them structurally aligned. Do NOT alter cursor()/transaction() or the `in_session` property.
